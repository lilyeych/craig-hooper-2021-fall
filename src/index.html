I’ve been a UX designer since before it was called that (7,357 days to be exact)

Flash, spacer GIFs, tables, system fonts... remember all that? If you do, you’re old—but back then you had to know how to code your own work, because that was the only way to get it done, and get it live.

The years I spent designing and coding my work were a complete education in digital product development: I understand how elements in a static design will map to the functionality of the finished product, and the implications of those choices on the user experience. If you're like me, and you understand what it takes to create a great digital experience, you know exactly the feeling of wanting to put your fist through your screen when you come across a poorly designed one. Users even less demanding than me still require reliable, consistent experiences; it's a factor that can help build confidence in a brand, or destroy it.

Simplicity, clarity, and a consistent user experience remain my core design goals. To that end, I believe in the use of design systems and pattern libraries to create a consistent logic and a user-friendly experience.

Banfield Agency, in Ottawa, Canada, is where I currently design digital experiences across a range of form factors and devices. Follow me on Twitter, Instagram or connect on LinkedIn.
